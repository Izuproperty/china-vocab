RMB Breaches 6.80 to Touch Its Strongest Level in Twelve Months
The offshore renminbi (CNH) broke through 6.80 against the dollar for the first time in a year, driven by post-summit dollar weakness, inbound equity flows from Hang Seng Tech index rebalancing, and Beijing's deliberate tolerance of appreciation during the de-escalation window.

Xpeng Rolls China's First Mass-Produced L4 Robotaxi Off the Line in Guangzhou
Xpeng began mass production of its X9-R robotaxi platform in Guangzhou — the first Chinese automaker to achieve volume L4 autonomous vehicle production — as China's AV regulatory framework accelerates ahead of US counterparts.

China vs US Autonomous Vehicle Deployment — Regulatory & Commercial Progress
Regulatory edge
China moves faster
Chinese municipal governments can grant L4 commercial licenses in 6-18 months; US city/state approvals have taken 3-5 years — regulatory speed is China's AV advantage
Technology platform

Xpeng's X9-R is a purpose-built robotaxi with 32 sensor arrays; XNGP (Next-Gen Pilot) software stack handles edge cases without HD map dependency

Xpeng operates the fleet and charges per-trip; targeting Guangzhou airport and convention corridors first — highest-density, lowest-complexity routes

Beijing's Three-Agency AI Agent Framework Enters Enforcement Mode
The CAC, MIIT, and MPS jointly issued enforcement guidance for AI agents — requiring registration, capability disclosures, and incident reporting for any AI agent handling financial, medical, or legal decisions for Chinese users.

Covered sectors
Finance, medical, legal
Agents that provide investment advice, medical recommendations, or legal analysis must be registered and assessed — covers most high-value AI use cases
Registration burden
60-day window
Existing deployed agents have 60 days to register; new agents require pre-deployment CAC assessment — higher compliance cost for startups than incumbents
Global parallel
Ahead of EU AI Act scope
EU AI Act covers high-risk AI systems but not agents specifically; China's agent framework is the first jurisdiction to explicitly regulate AI agent deployment at scale
