🔴 Core Risk
开源鸿蒙
Most Chinese robot manufacturers currently use NVIDIA Orin chips and ROS2 — both US-origin. An export control escalation targeting robotics toolchains is a tail risk that M-Robots OS and the OpenHarmony ecosystem are structurally designed to hedge. But adoption is unproven at scale.
🟡 Uncertain
地平线
Whether M-Robots OS 2.0 achieves the developer adoption necessary to displace ROS2 as the default is an open question. Ecosystem plays require network effects — Kaihong's partner list (Saite, Creality, DeyeeMed) is nascent, not yet representing the core humanoid players.
🟢 Actionable Signal
鸿蒙系统
D-Robotics (9660.HK) is a revenue-generating AI chip supplier — not a research entity. Its integration with M-Robots OS gives the platform a credible hardware anchor at launch. Watch D-Robotics's robotics segment revenue disclosures as a proxy for domestic stack adoption velocity.

🔴 Core Claim
外商直接投资
The −10.3% headline FDI decline has driven "China exit" narratives, but the contraction is concentrated in real estate and low-tech manufacturing — sectors already in structural decline. Using total FDI as a proxy for corporate conviction in China is methodologically misleading.
🟡 Uncertain
去风险
Whether the high-tech FDI growth is durable through a potential trade escalation is unclear. European companies expanding China supply chains in early 2026 may revise posture if US secondary sanctions on China suppliers tighten. The Stellantis-Dongfeng JV is announced but not yet operational.
🟢 Actionable Signal
脱钩
High-tech FDI at 40.4% of total — up from ~33% — suggests companies with genuine R&D and advanced manufacturing rationale are not leaving. The EU Chamber survey improvement (68%, −5pp saying China harder) adds corroborating sentiment. Sector-disaggregated China exposure analysis outperforms headline FDI readings.
