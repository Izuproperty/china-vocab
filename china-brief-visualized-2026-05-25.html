Supplier Payment Cycle Compression — Working Capital Impact
Days payable outstanding (DPO) shift by actor type · illustrative for 17-assembler cohort · S&P Global Ratings, May 2026
Assembler impact
整车厂商
Float removed as a working capital tool. Stronger OEMs (BYD, SAIC) absorb this through scale. Marginal players face direct cash pressure — the implicit subsidy of delayed payables is gone.
Supplier gain
供应链企业
Battery, seat, and electronics suppliers see DSO fall from ~300 to ~54 days. Multi-year vendor financing burden structurally relieved. Balance sheets improve without a single new contract signed.
Policy intent
政策导向
S&P reads this as deliberate consolidation engineering — Beijing tolerating short-term stress to produce a leaner EV sector. The key unknown: whether local government implicit support delays the inevitable.

Structural shift: AI capital formation rerouting
The real signal
结构性转变
This isn't just an index rebalancing. It's the completion of a capital formation reroute — US VC out, mainland institutional in — engineered by NDRC policy and executed through Hong Kong's public markets infrastructure.
The valuation question
估值可持续性
Both stocks are up 300%+ from January IPO prices. Whether mainland institutional capital can sustain these multiples — without the global VC discipline of competitive term-setting — is the unresolved question.
For international investors
外资视角
China AI capital formation is progressively rerouting toward domestic channels. This changes both information availability (less cross-border reporting) and governance dynamics around these companies.
